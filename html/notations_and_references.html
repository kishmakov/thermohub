Notations and References

Notations

Suppose we are provided with a simple fluid. Let this fluid has the following physical characteristics:

  • $p, \left[\text{Pa}\right]$ — [absolute] value of pressure
  • $n, \left[\text{mol}\right]$ — number of moles of a substance
  • $V, \left[\frac{\text{m}^3}{\text{mol}}\right]$ — volume of 1 mole of fluid
  • $T, \left[\text{K}\right]$ — absolute temperature
  • $R = 8.314462175, \left[\frac{\text{J}}{\text{mol} \cdot \text{K}}\right]$ — ideal gas constant
  • $p_c, \left[\text{Pa}\right]$ — pressure at the critical point
  • $V_c, \left[\frac{\text{m}^3}{\text{mol}}\right]$ — molar volume at the critical point
  • $T_c, \left[\text{K}\right]$ — absolute temperature at the critical point
  • $\omega, \left[0\right]$ — acentric factor of the fluid
  • $Z, \left[0\right]$ — compressibility factor of the fluid

Books

Pedersen, K. S. and Christensen, P. L. (2006). "Phase Behavior of Petroleum Reservoir Fluids". CRC Press ISBN:9780824706944.

Book could be considered as a good extension of Calsep internal survey and a nice cook-book on hydrocarbon simulation. Provides some experimental data.

Poling, B. E., Prausnitz, J. M. and O'Conell, J. P. (2001). "The Properties of Gases and Liquids". McGraw-Hill Prof Med/Tech. ISBN:0070116822, 9780070116825.

Comprehensive survey of the literature and methods in thermodynamics of gases, liquids and mixtures. Contains extensive and useful tables of properties of 460+ simple components.

Haile, J. M. and O'Connell, J. P. (2005). "Thermodynamics: Fundamentals for Applications". Cambridge University Press. ISBN:9780521582063.

Detailed and illustrative introduction into thermodynamics for engineers. Pretty neat both on form and spirit.

Reid, C. R., Prausnitz, J. M. and Poling, B. E. (1987); "The Properties of Gases and Liquids". McGraw-Hill Prof Med/Tech ISBN: 0070517991.

More like a textbook version of Poling Prausnitz and O'Conell books. Contains some table for $C_p^0$ computation by contributional method.

Landau, L. D. and Lifshitz, E. M. (1959). "Vol. 6: Fluid Mechanics." (in Russian). Pergamon Press. ISBN: 978-0-08-009104-4.

Detailed and explicit textbook with a good examples, motivating for understanding.

Peng–Robinson equation

Pure Fluid

General

Equation was presented in Peng and Robinson (1976) with some corrections made by the same authors in (1978). In what follows we are trying to stick to original notations, some of them are described here.

Peng–Robinson equation itself is the special version of the general cubic equation of state with $\nu = 2$ and $\mu = -1$: \[ P = \frac{R T}{V - b} - \frac{a(T)}{V^2 + 2bV - b^2} \] where

$ a(T) = \left[1 + \kappa \left(1 - \sqrt{T_r}\right)\right]^2 a_c $ $ \left[\frac{\text{kg}\cdot \text{m}^5}{\text{s}^2 \cdot \text{mol}^2}\right] $
$ a_c = [\Omega_a = 0.45724]\frac{R^2 T_c^2}{P_c} $ $ \left[\frac{\text{kg}\cdot \text{m}^5}{\text{s}^2 \cdot \text{mol}^2}\right] $
$ b = [\Omega_b = 0.07780] \frac{R T_c}{P_c} $ $ \left[\frac{\text{m}^3}{\text{mol}}\right] $

and $\kappa$ is a dimensionless parameter such that

\[ \omega \leqslant 0.49: \] $ \kappa = 0.37464 + 1.54226 \omega - 0.26992 \omega^2 $
\[ \omega > 0.49: \] $ \kappa = 0.379642 + 1.48503 \omega - 0.164423 \omega^2 + 0.016666 \omega^3 $

With new variables $A = a(T)P\left/R^2T^2\right.$ and $B = bP/RT$ it could be rewritten in terms of compressibility factor \[ Z^3 + (B - 1) Z^2 + (A - 2B - 3B^2) Z + (B^3 + B^2 - AB) = 0 \]

It means that the value of compressibility factor has two values: liquid and vapor.

Fugacity

Formula for fugacity coefficient of simple fluid, calculated via PR equation, has expression \[ \log \phi = \log \frac{\mathbb{f}}{P} = (Z - 1) - \ln\left\{Z - B\right\} - \frac{A}{2\sqrt{2} B} \ln \left\{\frac{Z + (\sqrt{2} + 1)B}{Z - (\sqrt{2}- 1)B}\right\} \]

Heat Capacity

Departure function of heat capacity, calculated via PR equation, has expression \[ C_p - C_p^0 = a''(T)\frac{T}{2\sqrt{2}b} \ln \left\{\frac{Z + (\sqrt{2} + 1)B}{Z - (\sqrt{2}- 1)B}\right\} + \frac{R (M - N)^2}{M^2 - 2A(Z + B)} - R \]

Difference between capacities, calculated via PR equation, has expression \[ C_v - C_p = \frac{R(M - N)^2}{2 A (Z + B) - M^2} \] where

$ M = \frac{Z^2 + 2BZ - B^2}{Z - B} $ $ N = a'(T) \frac{B}{bR} $

In the case of simple fluid following formulas for derivative of parameter $a(T)$ could be applied

$ a'(T)= \kappa a_c \left[\frac{\kappa}{T_c} - \frac{1+\kappa}{\sqrt{TT_c}}\right] $ $ a''(T)= \frac{\kappa a_c (1 + \kappa)}{2 \sqrt{T^3T_c}} $

Sonic Speed

According to the section about sonic speed, in case of PR equation, sonic speed could be expressed in analytic form as \[ u_s = V \sqrt{ \frac{C_p}{C_v M_w} \left(\frac{2 a(T)(V + b)}{(V^2 + 2bV - b^2)^2} - \frac{RT}{(V - b)^2}\right) } \] where $M_w$ os the molar weight.

Mixtures

Mixing Rules

Parameters of PR equation for the mixture of $N$ simple fluids where molar percent of the $i$-th component is $z_i$ are defined by mixing rules

\[ a = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \sum_{j=1}^{N} z_i z_j a_{ij} \] \[ b = \sum_{i=1}^{N} z_i b_i \]

Here $a_{ij}$ is expressed through empirically determined binary interaction coefficient $\delta_{ij}$ characterizing the binary formed by component $i$ and component $j$ as \[ a_{ij} = (1 - \delta_{ij}) \sqrt{a_i a_j} \]

Some $\delta_{ij}$ are tabulated (e.g. table 4.2 in Pedersen and Christensen(2006)). For those one which are not tabulated formula of Chueh and Prausnitz (1967) could be used: \[ 1 - \delta_{ij} = \left[\frac{2 V_{c, i}^{1/6} V_{c, j}^{1/6}}{V_{c, i}^{1/3} + V_{c, j}^{1/3}}\right]^n \]

Fugacity

Formula for fugacity coefficient of the mixture, calculated via PR equation and standard mixing rules, has expression (see Peng and Robinson (1976) or equation 4.65 in Pedersen and Christensen (2006) for more detail) \[ \log \phi_i = \log \frac{\mathbb{f_i}}{z_i P} = - \ln (Z - B) + (Z - 1)\frac{b_i}{b} - \frac{A}{2\sqrt{2} B} \left[\frac{1}{a} \left(2 \sum_{j = 1}^N z_j a_{ij}\right) - \frac{b_i}{b} \right] \ln\left\{\frac{Z + (\sqrt{2} + 1) B}{Z - (\sqrt{2} - 1) B}\right\} \]

Heat Capacity

Formulas for heat capacity departure function and difference of capacities for the mixture of simple fluids are the same as for the simple fluid. Corresponding changes for the expressions of derivatives of $a(T)$ should be done.

Since $a$ is the linear combination of parameters $a_{ij}$, derivatives of $a$ are expressed through derivatives of $a_{ij}$ straightforward

\[ a' = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \sum_{j=1}^{N} z_i z_j a'_{ij} \] \[ a'' = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \sum_{j=1}^{N} z_i z_j a''_{ij} \]

Derivatives of $a_{ij}$ in the case of time-independent interaction coefficients are given by formulas

\[ a_{ij}' = \frac{1-\delta_{ij}}2 \left[\frac{a_i'a_j + a_j'a_i}{\sqrt{a_i a_j}}\right] \] \[ a_{ij}'' = \frac{1-\delta_{ij}}4 \left[\frac{2a_i a_j (a_i'' a_j + 2a_i'a_j' + a_i a_j'') - (a_i'a_j + a_j'a_i)^2}{(a_i a_j)^{3/2}}\right] \]
